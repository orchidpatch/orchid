My Daughter Is Bees Now

dad i have something to tell you, my daughter said to me, and i braced myself for the coming-out conversation, having expected the eventuality since she was eight or nine, dad, she said, dad i am a hive of bees dad, she said, and i looked at those eyes that were both her mother’s and not her mother’s, and i said daughter i don’t understand, is this a joke? and she said dad, there’s nothing to understand, it is just the way i am, and i am a hive of bees. i laughed and when her eyes didn’t change i got mad. i get it, i said to her, it’s hard to talk about being gay, you don’t have to hide it with this stupid story, and she said, who i am is not stupid. then i saw something move beneath her eyes, and the flicker of wings in her pupils, and, i said, no matter what — 

        a glamour flicks off…
        there are eyes and then
        six bees replace them

i stopped saying anything but screams because, lo and behold, she was just bees now. how i hadn’t seen this before i don’t know. her body that i no longer could see as human spread out through the room and maybe it could still speak but i couldn’t. i guess now i know what kind of father i am because i just started bellowing and swatting around. thinking back, looks like they never stung me until i started hitting them, squishing them in my fists. when i came to my senses she was gone. 

        through the window
        October light slants through the blinds
        seventeen husks on the floor

i regretted the outburst immediately but had nightmares for weeks. her mother and i taking walks and then i’d be stuck in some web and bees would crawl over me, turning me to honey and mulch. i’d try to talk back but i don’t know how to dance let alone dance like how bees talk to each other. 

        how do you send a letter
        to a runaway?
        another weekend snowstorm

she weeks turned to months and foot after foot dumped down on me. i kept tabs on the credit cards she stole and bought a beekeeper’s mask and hoped not closing the cards was enough of a signal i was sorry but maybe not because she didn’t come back. what kind of love can bees expect from the world? 

        winter into spring in increments
        what kind of love can bees expect?
        are flowers alone enough?

nightmares turned into memory-dreams: her gold hair like her mother’s and how she would scream at the moon at night when i walked with her in my arms when she couldn’t sleep and how she’d insist on feeding herself soon as she could gesture for herself and i turned the yard into one enormous garden, as many flowers as i can fit, just in case that’s what she’s waiting for to come home. i’m sorry, i want the flowers to say, i’m sorry, i’m sorry, i’m sorry.

May. she returns.
it’s ok dad, she says, i’m me now.
some bees follow me inside, and hover over the couch; grab the remote like they’re a hand, and we watch tv until we fall asleep.

May 2015
